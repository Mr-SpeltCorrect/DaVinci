Sources

PRIMARY SOURCES

PRIMARY SOURCE 1

"Leonardo da Vinci, Mona Lisa." Wikimedia Commons, Wikimedia Foundation, https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Leonardo_da_Vinci_-_Mona_Lisa_(Louvre,_Paris).jpg .

This source provides the original image of Mona Lisa painted by Leanardo Da Vinci, and currently housed in the Louvre Museum. The painting is created using oil on a poplar wood panel and reflects Da Vinci’s mastery of materials.
PRIMARY SOURCE 2

"Technical Drawings by Leonardo da Vinci." Wikimedia Commons, Wikimedia Foundation, https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Technical_drawings_by_Leonardo_da_Vinci .

This collection provides original images of Leonardo’s engineering and invention sketches. From pulley mechanism designs, crane designs, and inflatable lifebelts, these sketches help demonstrate how he combined scientific observation with artistic design.
PRIMARY SOURCE 3

"Lady with an Ermine - Leonardo da Vinci." Wikimedia Commons, Wikimedia Foundation, https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Lady_with_an_Ermine_-_Leonardo_da_Vinci_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg .

This image of Leonardo’s portrait of Cecilia Gallerani shows his approach to detail and expression. Painted with oil and tempera on a wooden panel, this minimalistic image uses careful placement of light and shadow.
PRIMARY SOURCE 4

"Last Supper (Leonardo da Vinci)." Wikimedia Commons, Wikimedia Foundation, https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Last_Supper_by_Leonardo_da_Vinci .

This source provides the image to Leonardo Da Vinci’s famous mural The Last Supper. Depicting Jesus surrounded by his twelve disciples, this work stands out as a compositional masterpiece at the Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan, Italy.

SECONDARY SOURCES

SECONDARY SOURCE 1

Bambach, Carmen. "Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519)." Heilbrunn Timeline of Art History. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Oct. 2002, http://www.metmuseum.org/toah/hd/leon/hd_leon.htm .

This source contains a comprehensive overview of Leonardo Da Vinci, emphasizing his drawings and artistic endeavors. It details his scientific curiosity and his habit of observation, experimentation, and recording ideas through drawing. The source dives deeper into the stories and techniques behind The Last Supper and The Mona Lisa
SECONDARY SOURCE 2

Heydenreich, Ludwig Heinrich. "Leonardo da Vinci." Encyclopaedia Britannica, https://www.britannica.com/biography/Leonardo-da-Vinci .

This source gives a detailed biography of Leonardo da Vinci and covers his life, achievements, and contributions to art, science, and engineering. It starts by highlighting his upbringing in Florence, the impact his first apprenticeship had, and his early artworks. It delves into his dedication to the notebooks, his fascination with math and sciences, and love for artistic detail. Lastly, it covers his later accomplishments and life, highlighting his lasting impact in engineering and artistry.
SECONDARY SOURCE 3

"Leonardo da Vinci." Web Gallery of Art, https://www.wga.hu/html/l/leonardo/index.html .

This website provides much background information about Da Vinci’s works. Categorized across all disciplines, this source breaks down images and descriptions of his work in order to provide a clearer picture of what he accomplished in each area of expertise.
SECONDARY SOURCE 4

Murtagh, Leallyn. "Leonardo da Vinci the Engineer." USC Illumin, University of Southern California, https://illumin.usc.edu/leonardo-da-vinci-the-engineer/ .

This article focuses on Leonardo’s engineering ideas and inventions. It explains the pervasiveness of his inventions today and his approach to solving problems. From inventions like the worm gear, utilization of the flywheel, techniques to bend wood to make bridge arches, and invention of the robot, Da Vinci was truly ahead of his time.
SECONDARY SOURCE 5

"Thousands of Pages of Inventions, Ideas and Flying Machines." YouTube, uploaded by PBS, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GRqCChNvseg .

This video explains Leonardo’s notebooks and inventions, especially his flying machine designs. It tells how Da Vinci’s inventions were ahead of their time and continue to impact engineering today. Furthermore, it explains his pursuits of astrology, architecture, and flight.
