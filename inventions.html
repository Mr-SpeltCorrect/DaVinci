Inventions

A mind five centuries ahead of its time

Leonardo's notebooks contain thousands of sketches of machines, structural designs, and engineering concepts — many of which were not realized until centuries after his death. As Leallyn Murtagh writes, his ideas were remarkable not only for their originality but for their pervasive influence on modern engineering.

01

Flying Machines & the Ornithopter

Leonardo spent years fascinated with the flight of birds, filling dozens of notebook pages with observations of wing anatomy and the mechanics of lift and thrust. His most famous design, the ornithopter, was a human-powered flapping-wing aircraft and it was based on the same aerodynamic principles that is utilized in modern aviation. He also designed an early helicopter concept (the "aerial screw") and a hang-glider-like device.

02

The Robotic Knight

Around 1495, Leonardo designed what is now considered the first humanoid robot in history, a knight capable of sitting, standing, raising its arms, and moving its head and jaw. The mechanism was powered by a system of pulleys, cables, and gears operated from within the body. Modern roboticists reconstructed the design from Leonardo's notebook sketches in the 1990s and found it to be functional.

03

The Worm Gear

Among Leonardo's most practically impactful mechanical inventions was his application and refinement of the worm gear, a type of gear where a screw (the "worm") meshes with a toothed wheel (the "gear"). This arrangement allows large reductions in rotational speed and significant increases in torque, making it ideal for heavy lifting and precision machinery. The worm gear remains fundamental to modern mechanical engineering.

04

Utilization of the Flywheel

Leonardo figured out how to utilize the flywheel — a heavy rotating disk that stores kinetic energy and smooths the output of engines and machines. The flywheel is now a necessity in mechanical engineering. His conceptual understanding of rotational inertia was far beyond its time.

05

Inflatable Lifebelt & Diving Suit

Among Leonardo's lesser-known but strikingly modern inventions were designs for an inflatable lifebelt and a full diving suit equipped with a breathing tube leading to a bag of air on the surface. These concepts came before modern personal flotation devices and diving equipment by nearly four centuries.

06

Bridge Designs & Wooden Arch Techniques

Leonardo devised innovative techniques for bending wood to construct bridge arches without nails or other fasteners. He also designed a portable bridge for military use that could be quickly assembled and disassembled by a field army. His bridge designs remain remarkable for the structure and practicality.
